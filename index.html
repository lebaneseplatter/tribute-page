About

Max Emilian Verstappen (Dutch pronunciation: [ˈmɑks fɛrˈstɑpə(n)]; born 30 September 1997) is a Dutch and Belgian racing driver who competes under the Dutch flag in Formula One for Red Bull Racing.
Max Emilian Verstappen was born on 30 September 1997 in Hasselt, Belgium to Jos Verstappen and Sophie Kumpen. Verstappen's parents separated when he was young, after which he lived with his father. His younger sister, Victoria, lived with their mother. Verstappen has three younger half-siblings from his father: a sister from Jos's second marriage and a brother and sister from Jos's current marriage.
His family has a long association with motor sports: his father is a Dutch former Formula One driver, his Belgian mother competed in karting, and his first cousin once removed, Anthony Kumpen, competed in endurance racing and is a two-time NASCAR Whelen Euro Series champion currently serving as the team manager for PK Carsport in Euro Series. Verstappen initially attended a secondary school in Maaseik, before moving into private tutoring; he admitted that he used to leave class early to attend races across Europe with his father.

Career

Karting

Max Verstappen's Karting Career (2005–2013)

2005–2009: National Dominance Started karting at age 4, competing in championships by age 7.
2005–2006: Won Mini Junior and VAS Championships in Limburg, Belgium.
2007: Graduated to Rotax Max Mini Max class. Won Rotax Max Challenge Belgium and Dutch Championship.
2008: Racing with CRG kart entered by his father. Won Belgian Cadet Championship, Rotax Max Challenge Belgium, and Benelux Karting Series.
2009: Joined Pex Racing (CRG customer team). Defended Mini Max titles and won Belgian KF5 Championship.
2010–2012: International Breakthrough 2010: Signed by CRG factory team. Runner-up in KF3 World Cup (behind Alexander Albon). Won WSK Euro Series, WSK World Series, WSK Nations Cup, and Bridgestone Cup. 2011: Won WSK Euro Series (ahead of Esteban Ocon). Finished 14th in European Championship; taken out by Charles Leclerc in World Cup. 2012: Joined Intrepid Driver Program. Won WSK Master Series and South Garda Winter Cup (KF2). Finished 6th in WSK Euro Series; 8th in KF1 World Championship. Returned to CRG factory team mid-year. Runner-up in KF2 World Cup; 21st in SKUSA SuperNationals (KZ2).
2013: Record-Breaking Season Progressed to senior KF and KZ categories at age 15. Won three CIK-FIA championships: Two European Championships (direct-drive and gearbox classes). KZ World Championship (youngest-ever winner, beating Charles Leclerc). Finished 3rd in KF World Championship (disqualified in final race). Also won: South Garda Winter Cup (KF2) WSK Euro Series (KZ1) WSK Super Master Series (KZ2) Verstappen's karting career was marked by rapid progression, fierce rivalries (Albon, Leclerc, Ocon), and a trail of records that foreshadowed his future dominance in Formula One.
